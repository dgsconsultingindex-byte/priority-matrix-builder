Step 1: Set Up Email Parser

Use Zapier's Email Parser to create a dedicated email address (e.g., priorities@priobox.parser.zapier.com). Forward emails to this address to create tasks.

Step 2: Configure Parsing Rules

Step 4: Share With Team

Give your team the special email address. Anyone can now create priority tasks by forwarding emails!

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this work with Outlook/Microsoft 365?

Yes! While the examples show Gmail, Zapier fully supports Microsoft Outlook and Office 365. The setup process is identical - just choose "Outlook" instead of "Gmail" when connecting your email.

How many emails will I receive?

That's completely up to you! You control the triggers with filters. Common setups: 1-5 emails per day for task assignments, or 1 daily digest email in the morning. You decide what's too much.

Can I customize the email format?

Absolutely! You have full control over the subject line, body text, formatting, and what information is included. Make it as simple or detailed as you need.

Will emails go to spam?

Unlikely, since they're sent from legitimate email services (Gmail/Outlook via Zapier). However, you can whitelist the sender address to be 100% sure emails reach your inbox.

Can multiple team members receive the same notification?

Yes! You can send the same notification to multiple email addresses. In Zapier, just add the email addresses separated by commas in the "To" field.

Is there a limit on emails sent?

On Zapier's free tier, you get 100 "tasks" per month. Each email sent = 1 task. So you can send up to 100 emails per month for free. Paid plans offer unlimited emails.

Can I reply to notification emails?

With the basic setup, replies won't update PrioBox (one-way only). However, you can set up a more advanced workflow where replies trigger actions - this requires additional Zaps and some creativity!

How do I stop receiving emails?

Simply turn off the Zap in your Zapier dashboard. You can pause it temporarily or delete it entirely. You're always in control.
